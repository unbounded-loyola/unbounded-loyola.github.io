About Unbounded

Read our story to learn more about who we are!

Who we are

We are Loyola students with a calling to do ministry on campus to help students know Jesus. We are a multicultural Bible focused Christian group at Loyola University. We meet weekly to read and discuss a passage from the Bible to grow our knowledge and relationship to Jesus.

Our Mission

Our mission is to help students grow spiritually and become lifelong followers of Jesus. We're all about diving into the Bible, studying its teachings, and encouraging Bible journaling and writing testimonies. Our prayers extend to different countries, fostering a global perspective. Additionally, we're committed to nurturing leadership skills by leading small groups in our weekly Bible discussions.

Our story

In 2018, three Loyola students who had grown up in the church came together to meet weekly, reading and discussing the Bible (The NIV, New International Version). They extended invitations to friends and other students, urging them to join their gatherings at the IC every Thursday evening for an hour. Taking turns leading, they emphasized the practice of journaling Bible readings and prayers, as well as writing testimonial reflections to articulate thoughts and bridge the Word of God to their lives.

Recognizing the positive impact on students' closeness to God, these meetings evolved into a recurring event each semester. The focal point for attendees was the depth of their Bible studies, characterized by an open atmosphere where everyone felt free to share without pressure. The trio behind this initiative comprised Victor (Software Engineering major, class of 2022, from Madagascar), Justin (Biology major, class of 2021, from Chicago), and Rebekah (Literature major, class of 2022, from Korea). In 2021, considering their diverse backgrounds, they coined the name Unbounded, symbolizing the boundless nature of the Word of God. (PS: Victor created this website for his portfolio as a Software Engineering major ;)). They sustained these meetings every semester for four years until their graduation, but that wasn't the end of the group!

Other students who had joined over the previous years expressed a desire to continue the meetings. That's us writing this! In Fall 2022 and Spring 2023, we took the initiative to reserve a study room at the IC every week and facilitate the meetings. God blessed us with enough students to conduct three weekly small group meetings in the Fall, prompting us to pursue becoming a registered club on campus. Despite the graduation of many friends in Spring 2023, we're extending invitations to more people and currently hosting two weekly small groups this semester.

Our Key Verse
